From the Depths of my brain

Creating in a mentally stifling time

August 9th, 2020 by Gio

During the era of COVID-19, we are constantly stuck inside, and new experiences have become harder to come by. However, even in the hardest of times, it's still possible to create something new.

Being stuck inside can really be a doozy, but it's important to keep your chin up. While it may seem like everything around you is just shaping up to be the same bland day, it's important to see how each day is unique and each day can call to a unique moment.

Creativity is like a fire, you've got all the elements you need. The paper. The twigs. The driftwood. The pinecones. The logs. The lighter fluid. It's all laying there, waiting to spark up into a beautiful fire, as streaks fly accross the night sky. But... it's just... NOT. You pound the log with your fists of fury. You stomp on the logs with all your might. You smash the twigs to shreds. You pour your lighterfluid over the damp newspapers while screaming in anger. WHY WON'T IT WORK.

Sometimes, it's just the approach. You've got everything lined up, but all you need is that little spark for your beautiful flame of creativity to light ablaze.

Some steps to create your spark

Below are tips in tricks on how to get started while stuck in a mental block rut.

Create a library of knowledge

Read, watch movies, go the museum (this may prove challenging at this point in time), go on a hike, the beach, breathe in the fresh air, gain new knowledge online. Just expose yourself to as many things as possible

Connect knowledge and ideas

Get all those beautiful new experiences you've had and geoup them together. Brainstorm. What did you like? What didn't you like? Why? What are some things that made you feel happy or sad. Really ask yourself questions into why the epxeriences you had were the way you were, and if you could, what would you do to change them, or make your moments you had better. If you have a solution to problem, that's a great place to begin creating!

Form an idea out of your newly found connections

With the new connections and experiences you've had, try to form a concrete idea with them. Doesn't matter if you think the idea sucks, or if it feels like you're copying someone else. In the words of Koci (praise be) "everything is a remix". Therefore there is no shame in producing something similar to something someone else made. Just don't plagarise. But we are constantly drawing from different sources when we're creating, whether we like it or not

Work and rework until you're satisfied with your creation

You're not done yet! Now that you have something there's still much to be done. Keep refining your work. What can you adjust? What can you make better? What new ideas can you come up with out of the ideas you've already made. Don't view this production you've made be the end of your creative endeavour, but rather a springpad for much much more. Just keep working it, and every ounce of sweat your pour in will pay back (in the creative sense) big time!
