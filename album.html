Mental Unblock Album

Below I've attatched pictures of things I do to be able to get out of my creativity slumps. From biking, to drawing, to just getting out of the house, there are so many ways to stimulate your brain

Get on your bike and go out on an adventure! Where are you going? Who knows. Go straight, then left, then right... until you've lost yourself. Getting lost is one the best ways get your mind wandering

My friend Dana and I biking and doing some acrobatics on our way to the city.

Find a new place to look out onto. Hop a fence, follow a trail. Do whatever it is you need to do to get somewhere new! Don't be afraid to break some rules on the way

A beautiful view from the bluffs of Treasure Island, San Francisco.

Cook or bake something you've never made before! Getting yourseld down and dirty, knee deep in flour and butter and creating something from nothing can help jumpstart your next creative endeavour.

Some fresh croissants that hopped out the oven. I had no idea what I was doing but with some help, they came out great!

Draw on something unconventional. While that may look like drawing all over my walls for me, for you that might be drawing on canvas, or finally using those watercolors grandma bought you.

The walls in my room. I've been drawing on them for about 8 months now. (Guess I'm not getting that security deposit back.)

Doodle and listen to music. Let the music just carry you wherever it wants to. Sometimes day dreaming can be the key to opening that chest of big ideas you have

A doodle from my sketch book. I drew this in a coffee shop while I was in sweden last April. Drawing has always meant a lot to me.

Get crafty! Sew something, make a popsickle stick man, get glue and glitter all over your room. Even if what you make isn't up to your design standards, that doesn't matter. We're just here to brainstorm

3 out of my big collection of colored pants. I always like getting crafty with them
